MANCHURIAN

Incredients required:

For the Gobi (Cauliflower) Fritters:

  • 1 medium-sized cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup cornflour (cornstarch)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed to make a batter
  • Oil for deep frying

For the Manchurian Sauce:

  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornflour (cornstarch) dissolved in 1/4 cup water
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 2-3 spring onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1-2 tablespoons chopped cilantro/coriander leaves (for garnish)

Process of making manchurian:

Prepare the Gobi (Cauliflower) Fritters:
In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, soy sauce, red chili powder, salt, and enough water to make a thick batter. Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter, ensuring it's well coated. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once hot, deep fry the cauliflower florets in batches until they are golden and crispy. Remove the fried cauliflower florets using a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Set aside.
Prepare the Manchurian Sauce:
In a separate pan or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic, ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute. Add chopped onions and bell pepper. Sauté until they turn translucent. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, chili sauce, vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper. Mix well. Pour in 2 cups of water or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Stir in the cornflour-water mixture, stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Cook the sauce until it thickens to your desired consistency. Once the sauce has thickened, add the fried cauliflower florets to the sauce. Toss well to coat the florets with the sauce.
Garnish and Serve: Garnish the Gobi Manchurian with chopped spring onions and cilantro/coriander leaves. Serve hot as an appetizer or with fried rice or noodles.

Enjoy! 😀
